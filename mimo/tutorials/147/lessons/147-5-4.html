The <td> tag stands for table data because we can't just put text but also images, links, lists, and other tables in it.

<!doctype html>
<html>
 <body>
  <table>
   <tr>
    <td><img src=""></td>
    <td><a href=""></a></td>
   </tr>
   <tr>
    <td>
     <ol>
      <li>Walter</li>
      <li>Jesse</li>
     </ol>
    </td>
    <table border="1">
     <tr><td>Let's cook! </td> </tr> </tr> </td>  
    </table>
   </tr>
  </table>
 </body>
</html>
<html>
<body><table><tr><td><img src=""></td><td><a href=""></a></td></tr><tr><td><ol><li>Walter</li><li>Jesse</li></ol></td><table border="1"><tr><td>Let's cook!</td></tr></table></tr></table></body>
</html>
]]>

Whoa, what a bloated and useless web page! Nevertheless, it shows the variety of things we can throw into a table.
