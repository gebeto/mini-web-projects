Dictionaries are unordered collections of key-value pairs. Unlike other collections, they use keys to point to values.

dict = {"name": "Robin", "age": 42, "weight": 60}
dict.pop("weight")
dict.update({"height": 1.72})
print(dict)
{'age': 42, 'name': 'Robin', 'height': 1.72}

Aren't they great, those dictionaries?
